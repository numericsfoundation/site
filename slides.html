Lesson Slides

Structured slide presentations designed to support clear and systematic mathematics instruction. Each set of slides guides teachers through visual demonstrations, number models, and step by step explanations that help students connect quantities, numbers, and numerals while building strong conceptual understanding and fluency.

Kindergarten

Lesson slides introduce the earliest ideas of number. Students learn to recognize small quantities through subitizing, connect dot patterns to spoken number names, and begin understanding that numbers represent how many objects are in a set. Visual models, counting actions, and structured demonstrations help students build a clear connection between quantities, numbers, and numerals.

Grade One

Lesson slides develop a deeper understanding of number construction. Students learn that numbers are built from ones and can be composed and decomposed into parts. Instruction emphasizes counting, part part relationships, and the early foundations of place value through building tens and ones. Visual models support the transition from concrete representations to symbolic equations.

Grade Two

Lesson slides extend number understanding and arithmetic reasoning. Students explore larger numbers, strengthen place value understanding, and develop efficient strategies for addition and subtraction. Instruction focuses on recognizing number relationships, using structured models, and building fluency with symbolic representations.
